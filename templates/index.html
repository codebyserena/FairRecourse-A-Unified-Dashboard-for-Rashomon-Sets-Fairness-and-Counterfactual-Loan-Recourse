Research Insight

This dashboard demonstrates that while many models may achieve near-identical overall accuracy (forming a Rashomon Set), they can disagree significantly on individual predictions.

This means that a decision to approve or reject a credit applicant is not just based on their data, but also arbitrarily dependent on which "equally good" model was selected. This instability affects the counterfactual recourse given to rejected applicants and the fairness metrics across demographic groups.
